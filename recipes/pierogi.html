Pierogi
(from Leanne Brown's "Good and Cheap" cookbook pp.138-139)
ISBN:978-0-7611-8499-7

Description

With this batch-style recipe, you won't have to worry about
cooking or cleaning dishes for days on end! As with any dish
worthwhile, it takes an investment of time and energy upfront,
but the trade-off ratio is much better than that of working 5
days in a row just to have two days off. This recipe asks you
of two hours max and saves at least triple that down the line.

In any case, it's delicious, budget-friendly, and ideal for inviting
friends over for a pierogi-athon! Encourage a pot-luck style approach
for trying different fillings. So many flavors work with potatoes!

Ingredients

  • Dough:
    • 4.5 cups all purpose flour
    • 2 tsp salt
    • 2 cups yogurt or sour cream
    • 2 eggs

  • Filling
    • 5 russet potatoes, cubed
    • add salt & pepper to taste
    • 1.5 cups sharp Cheddar cheese, shredded

  • Add-Ons
    • 2 scallions, chopped
    • 4 cloves roasted garlic
    • 2 tbsp Dijon mustard
    • 1 tsp Cayenne pepper
    • 1 tsp Paprika

  • Serve with:
    • 1 tbsp butter
    • scallions, chopped
    • sour cream

Steps

  1. In a large bowl:
    Mix the flour and salt. Pour in sour cream, eggs, and 1 tbsp of water.
    Massage ingredients slowly and carefully together until it becomes a
    smoothe dough. It's okay if it gets a little sticky. Cover it with a
    towel or plastic wrap while you make the filling.

  2. In a pot:
    Pour in the chopped potatoes and add water with a pinch of salt.
    Place a lid on top and bring water to a rolling boil over medium heat.
    Watch the heat as you cook! Once boiling, you can remove the lid
    and let the potatoes cook for 20 minutes, or until tender. You can test
    them with a fork: if it pushes through like a bean paste, they're done!

  3. Drain the potatoes and add in the shredded cheese, salt, pepper and any
    of the add-ons you want.

  4. Mash the potatoes with an electric mixer, or use two forks if you don't have
    a mixer. Once the filling is ready, recruit some extra pairs of hands for help!
    The shaping of the pierogi takes time!

  5. Flour your countertop generously. Cut the dough in half, keeping one half covered.
    Flatten the other half with a rolling pin until it's about 1/8" thin. Using a cookie
    cutter or a cup of about 3" in diameter, cut circles out of the dough.
    Note: Put the scraps into the remaining covered half of the dough.

  6. Each circle of dough gets around one tablespoon of filling in the center.
    Fold the dough in half and press the edges together. Lay the pierogi on a floured
    surface and use a fork to finish sealing the edges. Keep going until all the dough
    and filling is used up!

  7. With all the pierogi shaped and ready to go, bring a pot of water to a boil over high
    heat. Note: Only add a dozen pierogi at a time. Let them cook until they
    rise to the surface, about one minute. Use a spoon to collect the cooked pierogi
    and cycle through the remaining batches (should be about 5 or 6).

  8. To freeze any leftovers, wait until they've cooled before putting them into a bag
    and into the freezer. They should keep for about six months.

  9. Optionally: you can fry the pierogi after they've been boiled. Melt the butter in
    a pan over medium heat. Flip them over every few minutes until they're a deliciously
    golden brown on all sides. Serve with scallions and a dollop of sour cream.
